A haunted house is a building that stopped communicating honestly with its inhabitants. Doors that open onto nothing, staircases that change their minds, rooms that remember things their occupants would rather forget. In interface terms: broken affordances, inconsistent state, and a memory model leaking into the presentation layer.

Maybe that's why haunted houses frighten us more than monsters do. A monster is an external threat; a haunted house is infrastructure betraying you. The thing you trusted to be predictable — walls, floors, the basic contract of shelter — has its own agenda now.

Every designer has shipped at least one haunted house.
