Every monster is a critique. Frankenstein critiques ambition without responsibility; zombies critique consumption without desire; ghosts critique unpaid debts. A monster is what happens when a culture fails to solve a design problem and the problem starts walking around on its own.

That's why monsters age better than heroes. Heroes respond to the virtues of their era, which expire. Monsters respond to the errors of their era, which repeat.

If you want to know what is badly designed in a society, look at what frightens it.
